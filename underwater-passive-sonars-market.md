<p><ul><li>Low Frequency</li><li>Medium Frequency</li><li>High Frequency</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Underwater passive sonars are classified into three market types based on frequency: low frequency, medium frequency, and high frequency. Low-frequency sonar can detect sounds over long distances, making it suitable for submarine detection and oceanographic studies. Medium-frequency sonar balances range and resolution, often used for anti-submarine warfare and mine detection. High-frequency sonar provides detailed imaging and is ideal for harbor surveillance, mapping, and close-range target identification, offering high resolution but limited range. Each type serves specific operational needs.</p></p>
