<p><p>The cervical disc replacement market features two primary types: metal on a biocompatible material and metal on metal. Metal on a biocompatible material involves the use of a metal core combined with a non-reactive substance, enhancing integration with the body's tissues while minimizing adverse reactions. In contrast, metal on metal employs both components constructed from metallic materials, providing strength and durability but may raise concerns regarding wear particles and inflammation. Both types aim to restore mobility and reduce pain in patients with cervical disc issues.</p></p>
