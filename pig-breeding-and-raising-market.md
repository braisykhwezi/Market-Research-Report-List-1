<p><p>Pig-breeding and raising market types encompass various production systems tailored to different stages of pig growth and market needs. In Farrow-to-Finish systems, pigs are born and raised to slaughter weight. Farrow-to-Wean involves breeding sows to produce piglets that are weaned and sold. Feeder Pig Production focuses on breeding to sell weaned piglets for further growth. Wean-to-Finish systems take weaned pigs to market weight. Seedstock Production emphasizes breeding stock for other producers. Purebred Production specializes in high-quality genetics, while Alternative Market Systems cater to niche markets, such as organic or heritage breeds.</p></p>
