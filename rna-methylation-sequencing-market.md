<p><ul><li>Radioisotope Incorporation</li><li>Thin-layer Chromatography</li><li>Mass Spectrometry</li><li>Differential Enzyme/Chemical-RNA Interactions</li><li>Bisulphite RNA Sequencing</li><li>Antibody-based Sequencing</li><li>Big Data</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The RNA methylation sequencing market includes several key types. Radioisotope incorporation involves labeling RNA with radioactive isotopes to detect methylation. Thin-layer chromatography separates RNA based on chemical properties for analysis. Mass spectrometry identifies and quantifies RNA modifications. Differential enzyme/chemical interactions examine how methylation affects RNA behavior. Bisulphite RNA sequencing converts unmethylated cytosines for precise methylation profiling. Antibody-based sequencing utilizes specific antibodies to enrich methylated RNA, while big data analytics enables interpretation of vast RNA methylation datasets for research advancements.</p></p>
