<p><p>The solar microinverter market is divided into grid-connected and grid-off types. Grid-connected solar microinverters operate in tandem with the utility grid, converting direct current (DC) from solar panels into alternating current (AC) for immediate consumption or feeding back to the grid. In contrast, grid-off solar microinverters function independently, enabling energy storage solutions in off-grid scenarios. This differentiation caters to varying consumer needs, from residential installations that utilize grid access to remote applications requiring energy independence.</p></p>
