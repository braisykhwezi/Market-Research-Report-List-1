<p><p>Membrane filtration in winemaking involves various techniques to enhance wine clarity, stability, and quality. Microfiltration and ultrafiltration are used to remove yeasts, bacteria, and larger particles. Nanofiltration targets smaller molecules, effectively reducing acidity and adjusting flavor profiles. Reverse osmosis concentrates flavors while reducing alcohol or sugar content. Other methods include cross-flow filtration and ceramic membranes, each offering distinct advantages in preserving wine's characteristics while improving hygiene and shelf-life. These technologies are integral to modern winemaking practices.</p></p>
