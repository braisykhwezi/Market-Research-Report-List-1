<p><p>Card-based electronic access control systems are categorized into two main types: networked systems and standalone systems. Networked systems connect multiple access points to a central management system, enabling real-time monitoring, user management, and reporting across an organization. They are ideal for larger facilities. Standalone systems operate independently at a single location, providing simple access control without centralized management. These are typically easier to install and maintain, making them suitable for smaller businesses or specific areas within larger facilities.</p></p>
